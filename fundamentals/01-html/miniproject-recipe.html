Instructions

  1. Prepare the Dough

    In a large bowl, mix the whole wheat flour and salt. Gradually add warm water and knead into a soft, pliable dough. Add oil and knead for another 2-3 minutes until the dough is smooth. Cover with a damp cloth and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.

  2. Divide the Dough

    After resting, divide the dough into 8 equal portions and shape them into smooth balls.

  3. Roll Out the Chapatis

    Take one dough ball and dust it with flour. Using a rolling pin, roll it out into a thin, round circle about 6-7 inches in diameter.

  4. Cook the Chapati

    Heat a tawa or flat griddle over medium heat. Place the rolled chapati on the hot tawa. Cook for about 30 seconds until bubbles start to form, then flip it over.

  5. Puff Up the Chapati

    Cook the second side for another 30 seconds, then flip again. Using a clean cloth or spatula, gently press around the edges to help the chapati puff up.

  6. Finish Cooking

    Cook until both sides have golden brown spots. If desired, brush with ghee or butter before serving.

Expert Tips

  • Ensure the water is warm, not hot, to achieve the right dough consistency
  • Letting the dough rest is crucial for soft chapatis
  • Roll the chapatis evenly to ensure they cook uniformly
  • If the dough sticks while rolling, dust with a little more flour
